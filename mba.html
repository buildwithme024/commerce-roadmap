What is an MBA?

MBA (Master of Business Administration) builds leadership, strategy, business decision-making, and management skills. It is ideal for students aiming for corporate leadership, consulting, startups, or high-growth roles.

Who Should (and Should NOT) Do an MBA

‚úÖ MBA is a great choice if you:

  • Want leadership, management, consulting or corporate roles
  • Are comfortable with presentations, teamwork & networking
  • Are willing to compete aggressively for internships & placements
  • Plan to build long-term corporate or startup careers

‚ùå MBA may NOT be ideal if you:

  • Dislike presentations, group work or people-facing roles
  • Expect the degree alone to guarantee success
  • Prefer purely technical or individual work
  • Are not willing to continuously upskill

Roadmap During MBA (Step-by-Step)

  1. Build strong fundamentals in finance, marketing & strategy
  2. Choose specialization by end of first year
  3. Do internships & live projects (non-negotiable)
  4. Build Excel, PowerPoint & data skills
  5. Network with seniors, alumni & industry professionals
  6. Prepare for placements from Day 1

MBA vs Other Career Options

  • MBA vs CA:
    MBA focuses on management & leadership, CA focuses on accounting & finance depth.
  • MBA vs CFA:
    MBA offers broader roles, CFA is specialized for finance & investments.
  • MBA vs MSc / Analytics:
    MBA is people & strategy driven, MSc is technical & data-focused.

Common Mistakes MBA Students Make

  • Choosing specialization without research
  • Ignoring internships & live projects
  • Focusing only on grades, not skills
  • Poor networking
  • Late placement preparation
